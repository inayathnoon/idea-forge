Veettukkar — Kerala's Home Services Marketplace
Exploration Session: 2026-03-26

RAW IDEA
--------
An application for house helpers in Kerala — coconut tree climbers, house painters, cleaners,
cement construction workers, plumbers, and electricians — who work on daily wages or half-day wages.

10 DIRECTIONS EXPLORED
-----------------------

1. Hyperlocal Job Board
   Workers list themselves; homeowners browse and contact directly.
   Core bet: supply-driven discovery, low friction for workers to join.
   Risk: homeowners must scroll and reach out; no real-time urgency.

2. Job-Request Marketplace ★ SELECTED
   Homeowners post jobs; nearby workers are notified and accept.
   Core bet: demand-driven matching, real-time notifications bridge both sides.
   Risk: requires workers to be responsive on mobile.

3. WhatsApp-Native Bot
   Job requests via WhatsApp to a central number; bot routes to workers.
   Core bet: zero install friction for homeowners (everyone uses WhatsApp in Kerala).
   Risk: hard to scale, difficult to track ratings and history.

4. Cooperative Platform
   Workers join registered co-ops; platform books through co-op admins.
   Core bet: trust via institutionalisation.
   Risk: co-op onboarding is slow; adds operational complexity.

5. Subscription Crew
   Homeowners subscribe for X hours/month of cleaning or maintenance.
   Core bet: predictable recurring revenue and worker income.
   Risk: workers with diverse skills don't fit a subscription bundle well for one-time tasks.

6. Day-Labour Exchange (Available Now)
   Workers toggle "available now"; homeowners see a live board of nearby workers.
   Core bet: Uber-style instant matching for same-day needs.
   Risk: requires workers to actively open app daily to signal availability.

7. Skill Certification + Trust Profile
   Workers earn micro-certifications (e.g., "Verified Coconut Climber"); homeowners hire by trust score.
   Core bet: trust and safety differentiation.
   Risk: certification process is slow to bootstrap supply.

8. Community Referral Network
   Homeowners vouch for workers; vouches become reputation.
   Core bet: digitise existing word-of-mouth.
   Risk: cold-start problem — no vouches until network exists.

9. Agency-as-a-Platform
   App operates as a licensed labour agency with commission per booking.
   Core bet: revenue-first model with platform liability protection.
   Risk: agency registration adds legal complexity early.

10. Emergency Services Fast-Lane
    Urgent jobs (burst pipe, fallen coconut tree) get priority routing and premium rates.
    Core bet: high willingness-to-pay for urgency.
    Risk: narrow TAM; hard to build a sustainable daily-use habit on emergencies alone.

TOP 3
------
1. Direction 2 — Job-Request Marketplace (SELECTED)
2. Direction 6 — Day-Labour Exchange
3. Direction 10 — Emergency Services Fast-Lane

SELECTION RATIONALE
-------------------
Direction 2 was chosen because:
- It directly mirrors how the problem is experienced: homeowner needs a worker for a specific date/task.
- Real-time notifications make the product feel alive and useful, driving repeat usage.
- Ratings accumulate naturally over job completions.
- Proven marketplace model (analogues: UrbanClap/Urban Company in India, TaskRabbit globally).
- Incorporating an "available today" flag from Direction 6 captures urgency within the same product.
